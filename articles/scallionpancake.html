Scallion Pancake Recipe By James Jongs
Software Developer @ James Jongs Design and Engineering
A software developer, amateur cook, and adventurer.

History

Scallion pancake is a savory pancake made from dough not batter like western pancake. It closely resembles a green onion paratha from Indian cuisine. The ingredients are simple flour, water, salt, oil and scallions. I remember making scallion pancakes with my mom on cold winter Saturdays. This savory treat is best enjoyed with a warm glass of sweetened soy milk.

Ingredients

  • 5 stalks of green onion
  • 2 cups of flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 3/4 cup of water
  • 2 tablespoons of sesame oil (optional)
  • Oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Chop all the scallions into 1/8 or 1/4 inch rounds.
  2. Mix salt, flour and water, adjust water and flour to get a dough that just comes together but is still very pliable and can be rolled.
  3. Roll out the dough into a 1/8 inch thick sheet.
  4. Brush sesame oil or regular oil across the dough’s surface.
  5. Spread the scallions evenly across the surface.
  6. Roll up the dough lengthwise.
  7. Cut 2 inch sections from the long roll.
  8. Now roll out the 2 inch sections individually to about 1/4 inch thickness. (You will get green onion piercing thru the skin it’s ok they will char nicely adding more flavor).
  9. You can stack the pancakes and make it more layered or you can just cook the each 2' round individually (you will still have a few layers from the log rolling process).
  10. Now put 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan and fry the pancakes on medium low heat.
  11. When the pancakes eventually brown about 2-3 minute flip them over and fry for another 2-3 minutes.
  12. Remove from pan and slightly salt again.
  13. Then let the excess oil drain on a rack or paper towel.
  14. Tear you pancakes apart and enjoy the flaky layers of scallion.
