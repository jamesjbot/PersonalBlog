A classic dumpling recipe By James Jongs
Software Developer @ James Jongs Design and Engineering
A software developer, amateur cook, and adventurer.

History

This classic recipe is the best beginner's recipe for dumplings.
The earliest historical reference to the dumpling harkens back to 225 AD, when General Zhuge Liang was said to have dammed up a marsh with dumplings on his war campaign.
I've never wasted my dumplings to damn up a marsh, but they taste great for family gatherings, special events, or an everyday appetizer. You can freeze them precooked and they will keep for 3 months.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 a head of Napa Cabbage
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 pound of ground pork
  • 2 stalks of scallions
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon of sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons of light soy sauce
  • 1 package of round dumpling wrappers

Instructions

  1. Chop Napa Cabbage into fine strands.
  2. Chop scallions into 1/2 inch rounds.
  3. Mix egg, meat, cabbage, scallions, seasonings and sauces in a mixing bowl.
  4. Put water in a second bowl to wet the dumpling wrappers to seal them.
  5. Take a dumpling wrapper and fill with 1-1/2 tablespoons of filling into the middle of the dumpling wrapper.
  6. Wet a finger tip in the bowl of water.
  7. Run finger tip across the outside edige on half of the dumpling wrapper.
  8. Pleated the dumpling, pressing in to the wetted side.
  9. Dumplings can be frozen or cooked immediately at this point.
  10. Add 2 tablespoons of oil to a frying pan on medium heat.
  11. Then add dumplings and fry for 2 minutes; until the edges of the bottoms start to show golden brown
  12. Drop 3-4 tablespoons of cold water into the pot and cover to steam for 5 minutes
  13. When steam has boiled away fry alittle longer 1-2 minutes till the dumplings easily release from the pan (do not force them off after steaming; they will tear)
  14. An alternative to frying them would be to boil them for 10 minutes
  15. Either way, let cool for a few minutes and enjoy with soy sauce or eat them plain,
